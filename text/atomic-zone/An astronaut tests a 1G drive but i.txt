A spaceship with constant proper acceleration of 1g (≈9.81 m/s², the acceleration felt inside the ship) follows relativistic rocket kinematics. Inside the ship, the crew experiences normal Earth-like gravity (1g "down" toward the floor) and proper time τ passes at the usual rate—their clocks, aging, and daily life feel completely ordinary. No sensation of "high speed" exists; relativity is only apparent when looking outside.The key formulas (starting from rest) are:Rapidity φ = (g τ)/c
Velocity: v = c tanh(φ)
Earth/coordinate time: t = (c/g) sinh(φ)
Distance traveled: x = (c²/g) (cosh(φ) − 1)

Here c/g ≈ 0.9687 years (a convenient scale). What the astronaut actually sees out the windows evolves dramatically as velocity and time pass (pure special relativity in flat space; real universe expansion and matter would complicate things beyond ~10⁹ ly, but the qualitative effects hold):Early phase (0–2 years ship time, v up to ~0.97c): The view is still mostly familiar but distorted. Stars begin shifting forward due to aberration of light (the entire sky appears squeezed toward the direction of travel). Light from ahead is mildly blueshifted (stars look slightly bluer and brighter); light from behind is mildly redshifted (redder and dimmer). You can still recognize constellations, but they’re squashed forward. The ship has reached the nearest stars (e.g., after ~1 yr you’ve passed Proxima Centauri’s distance).
Mid phase (≈5–12 years ship time, v extremely close to c, crossing the galaxy): Aberration becomes extreme. Nearly all stars in the sky now appear crammed into a small, bright circular patch straight ahead. The forward view is intensely blueshifted: ordinary starlight turns ultraviolet, then X-ray or even gamma-ray energies. The Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) ahead is boosted into dangerous high-energy radiation—without heavy shielding the ship and crew would be cooked by this “blueshifted heat bath.” Behind you, the sky rapidly darkens as starlight redshifts out of the visible spectrum into infrared and radio. The Milky Way’s structure is unrecognizable; you’ve crossed its full diameter (~100,000 ly) in only ~12 years of your life while 100,000+ years pass on Earth.
Late phase (20+ years ship time, intergalactic and beyond): The forward patch shrinks to a tiny, blindingly bright spot of ultra-high-energy radiation (all cosmic light from the entire observable universe compressed ahead by aberration + extreme Doppler boost). Everything else is black. Meanwhile, a Rindler horizon forms behind due to constant acceleration: the universe appears to fall toward an invisible dark plane that steadily advances from the rear. Objects approaching this horizon visually slow down, redden dramatically, and fade out forever. Time on distant rear objects seems to stop. Nothing behind the horizon can ever catch up to you again—even light. By 20 ship years you’re hundreds of millions of light-years out (far beyond the Local Group of galaxies). By 50–100 years you’re at incomprehensible cosmic scales (10²²+ ly), vastly exceeding today’s observable universe radius (~46 billion ly). The sky ahead is effectively a single lethal pinpoint of gamma/X-ray radiation; everywhere else is utter blackness with the rear horizon swallowing the cosmos.

Summary from inside the ship: Your watch and body say “100 years have passed—I feel fine, gravity is normal.” But outside, Earth and the entire history of the universe have aged 3.29 × 10⁴⁴ years. You have traveled a distance so vast it dwarfs any structure in the cosmos (in flat-space approximation). The view is surreal: a single ultra-bright, deadly radiation spot dead ahead; total darkness everywhere else, with a growing “event horizon” behind where the rest of reality has visually frozen and vanished. This is why constant-1g acceleration is the only physically plausible way (in principle) for a human crew to cross galactic or intergalactic distances in a lifetime—while the universe outside races ahead by unimaginable amounts.
